Finding — What customers can do

Features the old site never had

Not styling — capability. Things a visitor can actually use that change whether they call.

New only

A real coverage map, not a single pin

The old site embeds one static Google Maps pin — your office. The redesign plots all 24 towns and villages you actually cover, so a visitor in Wroxham or Long Stratton can see at a glance that you reach them, instead of guessing and calling a competitor who says so more clearly.

Rebuilt

The same real reviews, finally readable

Every review shown is a genuine review from your Google Business Profile — same names, same words, same 4.9★ from 154 reviews. We rebuilt the presentation: clear avatars, verified badges, and your actual certification badges (DBS, City & Guilds, Yell, the Top 3 Plumbers award) laid out so they read as proof, not clutter.

old site: 0 file-upload fields found in the contact form
New only

Customers can attach a photo of the problem

The old contact form has no file upload at all — we checked the live markup. The redesign lets a customer snap a photo of the leak or the pipe, compresses it automatically in their browser, and emails it straight to you as a real attachment — so you can often quote before you've even left the yard.

The case, in short

The old site tells people you're reliable. The new one proves it before they've dialled.

Nothing about the business changed — same number, same reviews, same team. What changed is whether the site does its one job: turning a stressed-out visitor with a leaking pipe into a phone call, in the ten seconds they'll actually give it.

A heavier, plugin-built page with no photo upload, one static map pin, and review data Google can't turn into star ratings is leaving calls on the table every week it stays live. The redesign fixes exactly those gaps — and it's already built.
